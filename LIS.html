About

The National Addressing System (NAS), spearheaded by the Government of Malawi through the Malawi Communications Regulatory Authority (MACRA) as a multi-stakeholder initiative, seeks to assign every property in the country a unique physical address through standardised addressing protocols, physical infrastructure, and an integrated Geographical Information Systems (GIS) database. Serving as a practical municipal implementation of this national framework, the Blantyre City Street Addressing Portal operationalises the NAS by integrating national addressing standards with local spatial data and planning processes.

Designed primarily as a public awareness and access tool, the portal enables residents of Blantyre City to easily locate and retrieve official street addresses. At the same time, it provides the Council with a powerful platform for conducting efficient digital property searches, supporting planning and development control, and facilitating informed decision-making through spatial data. By integrating public accessibility with municipal planning functions, the portal bridges the gap between the National Addressing System and day-to-day local government operations, significantly strengthening land administration, urban planning, and service delivery across Blantyre City.

At the core of the portal is a powerful search functionality that enables users to quickly locate street addresses using plot numbers, geographic coordinates (latitude and longitude), and Universal Transverse Mercator (UTM) coordinates. This feature addresses the longstanding challenge of property identification, eliminating the need for residents and stakeholders to physically visit the Council's offices to obtain address information. It also strengthens institutional continuity by reducing reliance on individual staff knowledge and manual records. In addition, the portal incorporates an intelligent route-planning tool that enables users to generate the shortest route between two locations or street addresses. The tool calculates the travel distance, provides an estimated journey time, showcases the route summary, and displays the optimal route on the map, making it easier for residents, visitors, service providers, and emergency responders to navigate the city efficiently.

The interactive mapping interface allows users to toggle vector layers and switch between basemaps, including Google Satellite, Hybrid, and OpenStreetMap, to achieve optimal terrain visualisation. Clicking on a parcel generates a pop-up displaying key attributes such as house number, street name, area name, district, and postcode. In the application, an individual can also pick a coordinate, measure distance as well as area using the measurement tool. The application also indicates the location of an individual relative to the map. While area and perimeter values provided are approximate, users are advised to consult the relevant offices (BCC, MHC, and Ministry of Lands) for official and precise measurements.

The street address database is continuously being updated and improved. As the system evolves, it is intended to further strengthen digital transformation efforts within the City of Blantyre.
